Menu

Ciel d'Or is a progression of rare and beautiful ingredients where texture, flavour and harmony is paramount. Continually inspired by nature, the creative process for chef Sookie st james begins in working closely with the farmers, fishermen, producers and artisans who cultivate bespoke produce exclusively for Ciel d'Or. For each dish, the growth of every element and the selection of every ceramic piece is carefully considered and crafted for its role in the dining experience. Ciel d'Or brings a personal, interactive passage for diners through Sookie st james' culinary evolution.

Appetizers

An appetizer is a small dish or serving of food served before the main course of a meal. Designed to stimulate the appetite, appetizers are often light, flavorful, and visually appealing. In fine dining, appetizers are carefully crafted to balance flavors and textures, preparing the palate for the more substantial courses to follow. They are often served in smaller portions to showcase creativity and allow for variety in flavors.

Main Course

refers to the central or primary dish in a meal, typically served after appetizers and before desserts. It is usually the most substantial part of the meal, both in terms of portion size and complexity of flavors. The main course is designed to be filling and is often the highlight of the dining experience, showcasing the culinary skills of the chef. It can include a wide variety of proteins such as meat, poultry, seafood, or plant-based options, and is often accompanied by starches (like potatoes, rice, or pasta) and vegetables. The choice of main course varies greatly depending on the cuisine, season, and dietary preferences, but it is generally expected to offer a rich balance of taste, texture, and presentation.
